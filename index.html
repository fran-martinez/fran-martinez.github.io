About Me

Summary

I am Francisco Martínez-Martínez, an Artificial Intelligence scientist from Valencia (Spain). I hold an international PhD in Biomedical Engineering from the Polytechnic University of Valencia (2014). I had the opportunity to develop part of my career abroad at Research Labs from Imperial College of London, ETH Zurich and Czech Technical University in Prague. I am passionate about Deep Learning, more specifically applied to Natural Language Processing (NLP) since I love the idea of creating machines able to “understand” us.

My curiosity and eagerness to learn leads me to try to improve my skills and knowledge on Deep Learning, Machine Learning, Data Science, Coding and Math. I am constantly reading and researching the latest state-of-the-art papers, models, technologies, etc., as well as developing my own personal projects.

Currently, I am part of Santa Barbara Smart Health, a company created in 2016 by Fresenius Medical Care, world leader in products and services for patients with chronic renal failure. What I value the most about my job is creating expert systems that assist physicians and improve patients’ lives.

Santa Barbara
Clinical Records Processor

Automatically extraction of structured information from clinical records

This project is aimed at automatically extract relevant information from free-text clinical notes written by the physicians. The system is able to detect entities of interest, assign them a class and find the relation with other entities. That is, the system extracts tabular information from free-text notes. Some of the relevant extracted information are laboratories, prescriptions, diagnostics, allergies, procedures, sign and symptoms, etc.

Santa Barbara
COVID-19 Search Engine

Neural Search Engine implementation to provide answers from COVID-19 scientific literature

This project was aimed at building a search engine to find specific answers to COVID-19 related questions. It has been developed with the idea of saving time when research must be done on scientific literature.

This tool differs from a regular search engine in the following aspects:

  • The search is exclusively performed on scientific literature about COVID-19.
  • Instead of giving a list of articles as a result, the search engine provides a list of specific answers to the question. A link to the article and the paragraph where the answer is found is also given to provide more context.
Santa Barbara
Drug Checker

Drug Checker: A tool to aid doctors during drug prescription

The goal of this project was to develop an alarm system that allows physicians to know whether a prescription is correct considering both the patient characteristics and the interaction with other drugs. The system uses drug information (warnings, side effect, indications and contraindications) that was automatically extracted from drug leaflets my means of the use of Natural Language Processing and Deep Learning. This information is used to build an expert system that assists physicians during drug prescription.

IDAL
SmartBioSim

Simulation of the Soft Tissue Biomechanical Behavior in Real Time using Computational Intelligence

Development of a new method based on Computational Intelligence (CI) for real time simulation of the biomechanical behavior of human in-vivo soft tissues. CI is used to learn the behavior of those tissues. The proposed methodology is validated in two case scenarios: the simulation of the mammographic compression during acquisition of X-ray mammograms and the simulation of liver deformation during the breathing process.

IDAL
Solutions for FMC

Intelligent data-based solutions for Fresenius Medical Care applications

This project is funded by Fresenius Medical Care Deutschland GmbH. The goal of the project is two-fold; first, providing technical consulting related to data analysis in the field of medical applications. Second, to carry out Natural Language Processing to extract information from medical texts.

CMP
Multiple Myeloma Detection

Detection of multiple myeloma from low-dose CT images

Multiple myeloma is a cancer primary affecting bone marrow. We automatically classify low-dose whole-body CT images by means of a probabilistic intensity model and supervised learning. We focus on the long bones, especially femur. So far, there is no automatic way to perform this analysis. This is a collaboration with the Faculty of Medicine, Charles University.

CMP
Malicious traffic detection

Malicious traffic detection using active learning

One of the main problems detecting malware is that labels are expensive and scarce. We tackle this problem with active learning, a subfield within machine learning where a classifier can perform better with less training labels. This is possible because the classifier is able to choose the most informative data and learn from it. This project is a collaboration with Cisco systems.

CMP
Analysis of Langerhans islets

Analysis of microscopy images of Langerhans islets

Development of automatic techniques to analyze microscopic images of Langerhans islets to determine their suitability for transplantation. This is a collaboration with IKEM.

LabHuman
Liver Biopsy Navigator

Surgical navigator that allows planning and guidance liver biopsies

Development of a navigator that allows the guidance and planning of liver biopsies. The system allows, by means of Augmented Reality and Biomechanical modelling, merging real images from the patient with the 3D volume reconstructed from the CT images.

LabHuman
Localization of breast lesions

Automatic localization of suspicious breast lesions in 3D breast reconstruction

The aim of this project was to aid radiologists during stereotactic biopsies, decreasing thereby the time required for this type of biopsies as well as the radiation delivered to the patient during the process.

LabHuman
Planning ring implantation

System for planning the intracorneal ring implantation in keratoconus patients

Development of a surgical planning application for the implantation of intracorneal rings to correct keratoconus. Keratoconus is a dysfunction of the cornea that results from the abnormal growth of collagen in its tissue.

LabHuman
AR Surgical Navigator

Augmented Reality Navigation for Laparoscopic Surgery

Implementation of an Augmented Reality system for assistance in abdominal surgery and developing a liver biomechanical model that allows intraoperative navigation to the surgeons during laparoscopic surgery of the liver.

This project seeks to develop a navigation system with laparoscopy based on two pillars: virtual/augmented reality and a liver biomechanical model. Its objective is to advance in the creation of technology that facilitates safer laparoscopic interventions and in accordance with preoperative planning.
